The Best Movie That I've Seen


Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse

The best movie I've seen recently has to be Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse. This movie was the sequel to Spider-Man: Into the Spider-verse, another excellent film. Thee film follows the life of Miles Morales, the spider-man of his universe, as he deals with the separation of him and his other spider-people friends. The movie explores his desire to be able to jump through dimensions to meet with his friends but ultimately culminates in how he wishes to escape the "canon" fates of spider-people. Miguel O'Hara, the leader of the Interdimensional Spider Coalition and primary spearheader of the "following the canon" ideology becomes a huge obstacle in Miles' plans for his future.

As someone that is very interested in art, beyond just the story i found teh visuals of the movie to be mesmerizing. Creating a world in which hundreds of different comic characters, all in varying styles, can coexist must have been a nightmare but the movie is able to do it beautifully. We can tell immediately when a character is in a different setting as the world around them changes and they become an obvious "fish out of water". As Miles traverses through multiple dimensions we are told through just the visuals just how much of an outsider he really is. This ability to tell the story through the visual style of the movie makes the twist at the end all the more exciting as the viewer is able to sense something may be wrong but only really understanding the issue as the main character realizes the problem himself.
